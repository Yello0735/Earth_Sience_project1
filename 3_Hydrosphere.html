The Hydrosphere describes all the water found on Earth, existing in its solid, liquid, and gaseous states. It includes oceans, seas, lakes, rivers, glaciers, ice caps, groundwater, and water vapor in the atmosphere; it is the driving force of the water cycle.

Hydrosphere

Oceans

Contain about 97% of Earth's water, regulating the global climate by the distribution of heat through currents.

Cryosphere

That part of the hydrosphere composed of water in its solid state, for example ice caps, glaciers, and sea ice. It plays a major role in reflecting solar energy.

Groundwater

Water stored underground in the soil or pores and crevices in rock, and forming an important reservoir of freshwater.
