The Geosphere refers to the solid Earth, encompassing the planet's interior structure, rocks, minerals, and landforms. It extends from the surface down to the center of the Earth and is responsible for internal processes that shape the surface.

Geosphere

Crust

Crust is the outermost solid layer of the Earth's surface. Continental and oceanic crusts form part of the crust and vary in thickness, with different densities.

Mantle

Mantle the thick layer beneath the crust; the semi-molten material of this layer drives plate tectonics through convection.

Core

These include the various innermost layers of Earth, which have liquid in the outer core, while the solid inner core is composed mainly of iron and nickel, elements that create Earth's magnetic field.

Lithosphere

Rigid outermost shell of the Earth, comprising crust and uppermost mantle; the layer that breaks into tectonic plates.
