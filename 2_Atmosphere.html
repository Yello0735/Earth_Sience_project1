The atmosphere is the layer of gases that surrounds Earth, acting as a crucial, multi-layered protective blanket for the planet. It is one of the four main spheres of the Earth, fundamentally connected to climate, weather, and life itself.

Major Atmospheric Layers

Exosphere

The outermost layer, from about 700 km up to 10,000 km. Gas molecules here are very sparse and can escape into space. This is considered the final boundary before outer space and hosts many satellites.

Thermosphere

Spanning from 80 to 700 km, this layer is characterized by extremely high temperatures due to solar energy absorption. The International Space Station (ISS) orbits within this layer.

Mesosphere

Located between 50 and 80 km, this is the coldest layer of the atmosphere. It is also the region where most incoming meteoroids burn up due to friction.

Stratosphere

Extends from 12 to 50 km and notably contains the vital ozone layer, which absorbs the majority of harmful solar ultraviolet (UV) radiation.

Troposphere

The lowest layer, extending from the surface to about 12 km. This is where all Earth's weather occurs and it contains approximately 75% of the atmosphere's total mass.

Ionosphere

While not a distinct layer like the others, this is an active region within the thermosphere and mesosphere where solar radiation ionizes gases, creating the conditions for auroras and reflecting radio waves.
